About Wikwemikong First Nation

Wikwemikong Unceded Reserve is an Anishinaabe First Nation located on the eastern end of Manitoulin Island in Lake Huron, Ontario, Canada. As an unceded territory, Wikwemikong has never signed a treaty with the Crown and maintains its sovereignty. The community is home to the Three Fires Confederacy — Ojibwe, Odawa, and Potawatomi peoples.

The community spans 156 square kilometers of traditional territory, including forests, wetlands, and shoreline ecosystems critical for cultural practices and food sovereignty.

Flooding risk in Wikwemikong is driven by rising lake levels and increased precipitation events. Shoreline infrastructure is particularly vulnerable.

What this means: Flooding risk is projected to exceed the danger threshold by 2050 under all scenarios. 12 infrastructure assets along the shoreline may be affected.
